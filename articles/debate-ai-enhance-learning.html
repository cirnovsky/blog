In 2022, the launch of ChatGPT astonished everyone. It was the first time that AI displayed its powerfulness in such a explicit way to humans. People found it interesting to play with such a super intelligent yet obedient "servant". However, things quickly went beyond fun. In 2023, Turnitin, a well-known education platform, revealed that over 3.3% paper were more than 80% AI-generated. That is 2.1 million papers in number [^1]. In 2024, Turnitin reported 3% papers of fully AI-written content[^2]. And so far in 2025, Wall Street Journal reported ~40% of middle/high school students used AI without permission and nearlf half of college students did so [^3]. Despite the statistics, some are proposing integration of AI into education system, claiming that it can enhance students' learning experience and engagement. [^1]: https://www.turnitin.com/press/turnitin-ai-detection-feature-reviews-more-than-65-million-paper [^2]: https://www.newyorker.com/news/fault-lines/does-ai-really-encourage-cheating-in-schools [^3]: https://www.reddit.com/r/Professors/comments/1hsvmj7/some_recent_data_on_college_student_genai_usage
