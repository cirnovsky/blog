A mysterious property of 2 × 2 matrices

A2 − tr (A)A + det (A)I2 = 0

or

λ2 − tr (A)λ + det (A) = 0

That comes from the Cayley-Hamilton theorem that states every square matrix over a commutative ring satisfies its own characteristic equation. The characteristic polynomial of an n × n matrix A is defined as pA(λ) = det (λIn − A), where λ is a scalar of the base ring.

Each entry in λIn − A is either zero or linear to λ. Therefore, pA(λ) is a monic polynomial of λ:

pA(λ) = λn + ∑ciλi

The Cayley-Hamilton theorem states that

pA(A) = 0

One use for the theorem is that it allows An to be expressed as a linear combination of the lower matrix powers of A:

An = −∑ciAi Also, it is noteatble that the roots of pA(λ) are the eigenvalues of A.
